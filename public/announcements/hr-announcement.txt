📝 SECTION 6: MANAGING LEAVE REQUESTS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This is one of your PRIMARY HR functions.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VIEWING ALL LEAVE REQUESTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: Go to Outages → Outage Requests
STEP 2: You can see ALL submitted requests (not just your own)
STEP 3: Use filters to sort:
        • All Requests
        • Pending (awaiting decision)
        • Approved
        • Rejected
STEP 4: Click on any request to see full details

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
APPROVING A LEAVE REQUEST
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: Go to Outages → Outage Requests
STEP 2: Click on a "Pending" request
STEP 3: Review the request details:
        • Agent name and email
        • Start date and time
        • End date and time
        • Outage reason
        • Remarks provided
        • Attachments (if any)
STEP 4: Check the calendar for conflicts
STEP 5: Click "Approve" button
STEP 6: Optionally add approval remarks
STEP 7: Confirm the approval

AFTER APPROVAL:
• Request status changes to "Approved"
• Agent receives notification
• Request appears green on calendar
• History is logged

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
REJECTING A LEAVE REQUEST
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

STEP 1: Open the pending request
STEP 2: Review the details
STEP 3: Click "Reject" button
STEP 4: Provide a rejection reason (IMPORTANT)
STEP 5: Confirm the rejection

BEST PRACTICES FOR REJECTION:
• Always provide a clear reason
• Be professional and constructive
• Suggest alternatives if possible
• Example: "Team coverage insufficient on requested dates. 
  Please consider [alternative dates]."

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HANDLING CONFLICTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

BEFORE APPROVING, CHECK:
1. Calendar for existing approved outages
2. Team coverage for the requested dates
3. Conflicting requests from other agents
4. Business-critical periods

IF CONFLICTS EXIST:
• System may show "Conflicting Agents" warning
• Review who else is out on those dates
• Consider team coverage requirements
• May need to reject with explanation

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OVERRIDE REQUESTS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Sometimes agents request to override a conflict:
• You'll see the override reason provided
• Evaluate if the override is justified
• Check business impact
• Approve or reject based on needs
